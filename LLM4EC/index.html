LLM4EC

Large Language Models Meet Evolutionary Computation

WCCI 2026 Special Session — Towards Synergistic Intelligence
Organisers: Xingyu Wu, Yuejiao Gong, Liang Feng, Kay Chen Tan

IEEE WCCI 2026 — CEC Track Format: 2-hour special session with interactive panel & co-creation roundtable
Session abstract

The convergence of large language models (LLMs) and evolutionary computation (EC) opens new research directions where language-based reasoning and open-ended search complement each other. This session explores bidirectional synergies: how LLMs can act as intelligent operators, algorithm designers, and reasoning modules for EC; and how EC can optimize prompts, architectures and the lifecycle of foundation models. We invite contributions that bridge theory, algorithm design, evaluation, and applications (AutoML, NAS, robotics, scientific discovery, software engineering, etc.).

Committed papers (drafts / placeholders)

Below are representative titles/abstracts that the organisers will submit or coordinate. These are sample abstracts for the proposal — final submissions will follow the conference review process.

1. LLM-Driven Evolutionary Operators for Constrained Optimization
Xingyu Wu, et al.

This work studies language-model-based crossover and mutation operators for constrained continuous optimization, introducing problem-aware prompts and a hybrid evaluator that blends surrogate models with constraint repair mechanisms. Results show improved sample efficiency on benchmark constrained problems.

2. Evolutionary Prompt Engineering via Multiobjective Search
Yuejiao Gong, et al.

We present a multiobjective evolutionary framework that searches for instruction prompts balancing performance, robustness and compactness. Experiments on classification and code-generation tasks demonstrate improved generalization and transfer.

3. Architecture Co-Evolution for Efficient Foundation Models
Liang Feng, et al.

This paper proposes an evolutionary NAS that co-evolves layer-wise configurations and training schedules for compact foundation models, using performance-cost Pareto optimization and surrogate predictors to accelerate the search.

4. Explainable EC–LLM Hybrids for Scientific Discovery
Collaborative Team

We integrate causal probing with language-guided mutation operators to discover interpretable models in a chemistry optimization task, showing discoveries that are both high-performing and human-interpretable.

5. Co-Creation in the Loop: Human–Model Evolution for Robotics
Kay Chen Tan, et al.

We propose a co-creative evolutionary framework where human feedback and LLM-generated behavioral priors jointly guide robot morphology and controller evolution. User studies and sim-to-real tests validate the framework.

Session format & review

Planned duration: 2 hours. Format includes 5 paper presentations (15 min each), a 20-min mini-panel, and a 25-min interactive co-creation roundtable with live audience contribution and a summary to be published on the session page.

Special activities (feature)
